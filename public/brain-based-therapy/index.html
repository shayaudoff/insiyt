01 — Neurofeedback

Train Your Brain to Self-Regulate

A non-invasive, drug-free therapy that teaches the brain to produce healthier brainwave patterns — improving focus, calm, emotional regulation, and sleep.

01

Brainwave Measurement

Electrodes placed on the scalp detect and measure brainwave activity using EEG (electroencephalography) — creating a real-time map of your brain's electrical patterns.

02

Feedback Loop

The measured brainwave activity is fed back to you in real time, often through visual or auditory cues — such as a video game or music that responds to your brain states.

03

Brain Training

As you learn to regulate brainwaves in response to the feedback, your brain begins to self-regulate — generating healthier patterns that persist long after sessions end.

Potential Uses & Benefits
🧠

Mental Health Conditions

Used to treat ADHD, anxiety, depression, PTSD, and addiction by training the brain toward healthier baseline states.

⚡

Cognitive Function

Enhances focus, memory, and learning abilities — helping the brain work at its natural best without medication.

🌙

Sleep & Pain

Explored for sleep disorders, pain management, and other conditions where the nervous system needs retraining.

🛡️

Non-Invasive & Safe

No medication, no surgery. Neurofeedback works with your brain's own electrical activity — safe for all ages.

02 — Integrative Psychiatry

Beyond Symptom Management

A whole-body approach that identifies and addresses the root causes of mental illness — genetic, biochemical, nutritional, hormonal, and environmental.

The INSIYT Approach

Integrative psychiatry is a whole-body approach to treating psychiatric conditions like depression, anxiety, insomnia, and ADHD. Rather than relying solely on pharmaceuticals to manage symptoms, the goal is to identify the root causes of mental illness in order to treat and restore the health of the body and brain.

Root causes are unique to each patient — they may be genetic, biochemical, nutritional, hormonal, or environmental. Integrative psychiatry targets these root causes, along with the underlying chemistry affecting brain and behavior, to promote lasting healing and recovery.

What to Expect

Initial assessment includes a comprehensive psychiatric evaluation with relevant blood work. Treatment plans address contributing factors including lifestyle interventions, nutritional change and supplementation, and medication management when indicated.

At INSIYT we offer research-backed interventions to identify key nutritional deficiencies, bridge the gap between biology and psychology, and facilitate real, lasting recovery.

03 — Brainspotting

Where You Look Is Where You Heal

An innovative, brain-based therapy that uses eye position to unlock and process deeply stored trauma and emotions — often reaching what years of talk therapy could not.

The Origin Story

Brainspotting was founded in 2003 by Dr. David Grand while working with a 16-year-old figure skater struggling to land a triple loop. During an EMDR session, he noticed her eyes wobbled at a specific point — and instinctively held her gaze there.

For ten minutes, she simply stared at that spot. What followed was the deepest emotional processing she had ever experienced — not just about skating, but about her parents' divorce and years of intense pressure. She later said those ten minutes were the most powerful therapy she had ever received. Afterward, she landed her triple loop.

How Brainspotting Works

Where you look influences how you feel — and becomes an entrance to where trauma is stored in the brain.

It is a flexible, patient-led approach that allows the brain to naturally heal itself at its own pace.

Brainspotting helps individuals access and process deeply stored trauma and emotions by focusing on specific eye positions.

It can benefit those struggling with trauma, anxiety, depression, dissociation, performance issues, and creative blocks.

If you've ever felt stuck in therapy — talking about the same things but never getting to the root of it — you're not alone. Sometimes trauma and emotional pain are stored deep in the brain in ways we can't access through words alone.

04 — Ketamine Assisted Psychotherapy

Non-Ordinary States, Extraordinary Change

Ketamine-assisted psychotherapy has been described as "a turbocharged method of reaching your mental health goals" — offering new hope for those whose depression has not responded to other treatments.

How It Works

The Science of Neuroplasticity

Ketamine works primarily by blocking NMDA receptors on a particular type of neuron, producing a surge of glutamate. This excess of glutamate stimulates the brain, enhancing neuroplasticity — the brain's ability to make new connections, create new neurons, and activate its learning centers.

These neurobiological changes, combined with the non-ordinary state of consciousness, increase the ability to radically transform your life. Ketamine is safe, legal, and considered a gentler mind-altering agent compared to other psychedelics.

The Experience

Holding Up a Mirror

The ketamine experience has been described as "holding up a mirror to yourself." The medicine simply illuminates and reveals what the mind is holding to be true.

When faced with one's shadow, self-incriminations, or hidden inner struggles, one is also gifted a hefty dose of self-compassion — more objective awareness of inner process and behavior, without fear or self-judgement.

The Outcome

Deeper Self-Acceptance

Many intense emotions may come up in meeting these parts of the self — and yet clients consistently emerge with deeper and truer self-acceptance. The sessions are supported by Shaindel's somatic and psychiatric expertise throughout.
