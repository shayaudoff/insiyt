What Is a Somatic Approach?

Somatic Attachment Therapy

Soma — Known From Within

Soma is a Greek word for "the living body known from within." This knowing connects us to our wholeness. Somatic therapy is an experiential approach towards mind and body integration — it repairs where there have been ruptures that leave a sense of fragmentation.

The pain, overwhelm, and coping responses manifested by sudden or complex trauma take us away from feeling at home in our body, at home with other people, or at home within the world. Somatic therapies bring us back. When we know where home is — and come to love the home of sensation and movement — we venture into the world with greater authenticity and increased vitality.

Who May Benefit

"Are the hours spent reflecting barely scratching the surface of the emptiness, aloneness, or numbness you feel inside?"

If talk therapy alone feels incomplete, access to what is stored in the deep layers of your tissues may allow a deeper movement toward profound healing. Somatic therapy offers a softening of the armor — an entrance into the safety that can only be found on the inside.



Therapeutic Touch Modalities

Healing Touch — Safety Back to the Body

Healing touch is remarkably helpful to regulate the nervous system and bring safety back to the body. Co-regulation through touch is part of what occurs in healthy human development. Methods that use touch to support co-regulation focus on presence and connection — addressing the ways in which both physical and emotional pain are held in our physiology.

Therapeutic touch techniques such as TEB and Havening are particularly beneficial at reaching the effects of pre-verbal, early childhood attachment wounds. Our earliest language is that of felt sensation. One of the greatest results of co-regulating therapeutic touch is the experience of "somatic trust" — a felt sense of safety that becomes a reference point for deepening self-trust and comfort in relationships with others and the world.

TEB

Transforming the Experience-Based Brain

A biophysiological approach to healing developed by Stephen J. Terrell, PsyD, SEP — a world-leading authority on Developmental Trauma. TEB supports healing from the impact of adverse childhood experiences at a biophysiological level.

Adverse childhood experiences can change the wiring of our bodies and brain when we get locked into "survival physiology." TEB gently works with these adaptive defenses to restore safety and choice.

HAV

Havening Touch Techniques

A psychosensory approach to healing trauma and other mental health challenges. Havening uses gentle touch to alter the electrochemical environment of the brain — depotentiating traumatically encoded memories and reducing their emotional charge.

Particularly effective for anxiety, phobias, PTSD, and stress responses stored below conscious awareness.

WHY

Why Touch-Based Healing Works

Defensive accommodations — persistent behaviors, beliefs, or states that negatively impact life — often underpin many of the things that drive people to seek therapy. Touch-based methods reach these defenses where words cannot.

Through somatic trust, a person accesses — perhaps for the first time — a felt sense of safety that anchors lasting change.

Movement Groups

Healing Through Movement

Movement-based group offerings that process emotion, build nervous system regulation, and reconnect you to vitality — through yoga, dance, and the rhythm of drumming.

SIYT Yogatherapy

Somatic Immersion Yoga Therapeutics

Yoga is a practice of inhabiting the body. It is a "bottom-up" approach that awakens sensation and awareness of feelings — opening the door to feeling what you feel and through that, knowing what you know.

The style offered through SIYT is vinyasa — linking breath with movement in a continuous flow. Each session is designed to encourage supportive reflection, mindful presence, and the safety to listen inwardly.

  • Creative sequencing with generous use of props
  • Meditative breath-linked movement flow
  • Somatic immersion — absorption into body sensation
  • Tools that translate directly off the mat
  • Simple, accessible techniques that restore agency

What Does It Mean?

To Somatically Immerse

"It's like rain that moistens the earth — an absorption and blending of non-physical consciousness and the sensation of the soma."

It's a sinking into the ground, or a filling-out of the container of the body. The felt experience may be that of being held or contained by the physical qualities of one's experience.

Through awareness and activation of sensation within movement, we identify more with our own physicality — tissues, organs, blood flow, and energy channels felt from within. This immersion leads to a sense of embodied presence that medications and talk therapy alone cannot provide.

Movement & Expression

Dance Therapy

Rhythm, Movement, Healing

Dance therapy is a form of somatic therapy that combines rhythm and movement to promote emotional healing. It uses a group approach to improve socialization in a safe space of authentic and spontaneous expression.

Dance therapists use techniques including mirroring, attunement, developmental phases, and "attachment dance" — helping clients progress through mental roadblocks, regressions, and delays in personal development and relationships.

  • Self-expression and emotional regulation
  • Building self-esteem and confidence
  • Trust and connection within the group
  • Understanding your own and others' feelings

Why Dance Heals

"Dance therapy helps individuals improve overall well-being by addressing a range of physical, mental, and social issues."

Many of these exercises are helpful in treatment because they build trust and connection between individuals and within the group — creating an environment where authentic expression becomes the path to healing.

Rhythm & Community

Drumming Circles

Can Drumming Help You Heal?

In mental health therapy, drum circles have been identified as a way of reducing stress, improving communication, and bringing people together. Drumming is a form of music therapy — rhythmic in nature — making it accessible for participants to engage in creative activities within the safety of a group session.

It can be especially helpful for people with anxiety disorder, depression, and PTSD, since drumming brings the mind to focus. Drumming also triggers the brain to produce endorphins — the body's natural chemicals that help one feel good and even elicit a feeling of euphoria.

  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves focus and presence
  • Erases loneliness through group connection
  • Triggers release of natural endorphins
Evidence-Based Practice

Healing Rhythms Therapy

Healing rhythms drumming therapy uses the power of rhythm and drumming to promote physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being. It is an evidence-based therapeutic activity used in mental health therapy, rehabilitation, and with veterans.

A drum circle is also a collective activity that erases loneliness — participants are connected with others, which can be incredibly important for people with mental health disorders. The shared rhythm creates a container of safety where self-expression, stress reduction, and genuine connection all become possible.

Helpful for: Trauma, addiction, grief, stress, PTSD, anxiety, depression, and social isolation.
