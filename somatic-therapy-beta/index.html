01 — Somatic Attachment Psychotherapy

When Talking Isn't Enough

Feeling stuck despite years of therapy — barely scratching the surface of the emptiness, aloneness, or numbness inside? Access to what is stored in the deep layers of your tissues may allow a deeper movement toward profound healing.

Somatic therapy is an experiential approach towards mind and body integration — it repairs where there have been ruptures that leave a sense of fragmentation. The pain, overwhelm, and coping responses manifested by trauma take us away from feeling at home in our body, at home with other people, or at home within the world.

Somatic therapies bring us back. When we know where home is — and come to love the home of sensation and movement — we venture into the world with greater authenticity and increased vitality.

Just as cellular energy is stored on a cellular level, our moods, attitudes, and habitual behaviours are stored in patterns of tension. Somatic therapy offers a softening of the armour — an entrance into the safety found only on the inside.

02 — Therapeutic Touch Modalities

Co-Regulation Through Touch

Therapeutic touch reaches the effects of pre-verbal, early childhood attachment wounds — restoring somatic trust as a felt reference point for all relationships.

TEB

Transforming the Experience-Based Brain

A biophysiological approach by Stephen J. Terrell, PsyD, SEP — healing adverse childhood experiences at a biophysiological level. TEB gently works with adaptive defences locked in "survival physiology" to restore safety and choice.

[ Image or video ]
Havening

Havening Touch Techniques

A psychosensory approach using gentle touch to alter the electrochemical environment of the brain. The greatest result of co-regulating touch is "somatic trust" — a felt sense of safety that becomes a lasting reference point for self and others.

SIYT — Somatic Immersion Yoga Therapeutics

Inhabiting the Body

[ SIYT yoga image ]
SIYT Movement Website ↗

Yoga is a practice of inhabiting the body — a "bottom-up" approach that awakens sensation and awareness of feelings. It opens the door to feeling what you feel and through that, knowing what you know.

The style offered through SIYT is vinyasa — linking breath with movement in a continuous flow. Each session encourages supportive reflection, mindful presence, and the safety to listen inwardly.

"It's like rain that moistens the earth — an absorption and blending of non-physical consciousness and the sensation of the soma."

  • Meditative breath-linked movement flow
  • Somatic immersion — absorption into body sensation
  • Tools that translate directly off the mat

Dance Therapy

Rhythm, Movement, Healing

Dance therapy combines rhythm and movement to promote emotional healing — using a group approach to foster authentic expression in a safe space.

Dance therapists use techniques including mirroring, attunement, developmental phases, and "attachment dance" — helping clients progress through mental roadblocks, regressions, and delays in personal development and relationships.

Many of these exercises build trust and connection between individuals and within the group — creating an environment where authentic expression becomes the very path to healing.

  • Self-expression and emotional regulation
  • Building self-esteem and confidence
  • Trust and connection within the group
  • Understanding your own and others' feelings

Drumming Circles

Can Drumming Help You Heal?

Drum circles reduce stress, improve communication, and build genuine connection — an evidence-based form of music therapy that is rhythmic, accessible, and grounding.

[ Drumming circles image or video loop ]
🧘

Focus & Presence

Drumming brings the mind to focus — especially helpful for anxiety, depression, and PTSD where presence is hard to access.

⚡

Endorphin Release

A physical activity that triggers the brain to produce endorphins — the body's natural chemicals that help you feel good and even elicit a feeling of euphoria.

🤝

Community Connection

A drum circle erases loneliness — participants are connected through shared rhythm, which can be incredibly important for people with mental health conditions.

🌿

Evidence-Based

Used in mental health therapy, rehabilitation, and with veterans — for trauma, addiction, grief, stress, and social isolation.
